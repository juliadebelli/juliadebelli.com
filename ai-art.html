Hello, yes this is Julia

Welcome to my ted talk. I'm a designer, tech enthusiast and former game artist looking to collaborate with AI art enterprises.

I started my online presence back in 2007, in a Tibia art board.

I was heavily influenced by early Ragnarok Online concept art and manga artists such as Kishimoto, Arakawa and Togashi.

During this time, I mostly used MSPaint and then Gimp, and no wacom, all mouse.

As I turned 14 and got a better grasp of English, I taught myself how to use Photoshop and Illustrator, then got down to honing my fundamentals: anatomy, perspective, light and shadow...

During this time, I was graced with the opportunity to become a resident artist for TibiaBR.com. I illustrated news, articles and moderated the art board where I had taken my first steps as a digital artist.

In 2014, I started my BA in Design at UFPE and kept making art for Tibia-related clients.


On October 2015, I joined Manifesto Games as an intern in 2D and 3D art.

Up until then, I had been focusing on becoming a concept artist.

But I put a pause on that when I was accepted into Apple Developer Academy, and switched my focus to tech-centered design capabilities (UI development, Xcode and Swift, prototyping). Quit drawing altogether for the next few years.

Back in 2015, I was shown a 'neural network' on an article, that composed any picture as if Van Gogh had painted them. I was both mesmerized and terrified.

Fast forward to 2022, soon as I got my hands on AI art generators open to the public, I started playing with it like a toy. My first ever prompt was 'ukyioe mount rushmore' and I was not disappointed.
