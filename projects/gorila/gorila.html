Stocks Viewer Feature: Enhancing Retention during Trade Hours

In this UX case study, we will delve into the design process of a Stocks Viewer feature for an Investments App. The goal of this project is to create a seamless and user-friendly experience for users to monitor and manage their stock portfolios within the app.

Problem Statement

Gorila is an investment management app marketed to a market-savy, Brazilian userbase. It first came to be when a group of fund managers decided to make into a web-app the tools they had built internally and used to fetch and calculate data in their clients' portfolio.

Investors usually have diverse types of assets purchased all over the place, so Gorila centralizes all portfolios and shows the user both general overview of all assets, to different classes and expanded views of overtime variation of prices.

The platform consisted of a React web-app for desktop users, and a mobile React-native app that came later but had fewer features than its counterpart. Most of the work I had done so far had been adapting desktop-made views to mobile, until they were on par.

Live Market Data was the first feature ever to be commissioned exclusively for the mobile app. It was also the first novel feature I was leading production, as I got the opportunity to draw it from wireframe to Zeplin, also adding it to the existing design system and documentation.



Research Phase

User Research

I approached finance professionals, in-person and on Whatsapp (n≈20) and gathered a list of 5 apps they used to check the price of stocks during trading hours.

They were

TradeMap (main competition)
Investing.com
Bloomberg
Yahoo Finance
Native Stocks Apps (Google, iOS)


Competitive Analysis

We analyzed the above-mentioned services to identify what's familiar to the userbase, and what could be opportunities to deliver an improved experience.


Ideation and Design

Use cases

Based on our research, we found 2 main user flows that the feature should appeal to

Cabin: User will pick a couple, but no more than a dozen favorite stocks to focus their attention
Grandstand: User will watch multiple stocks in a zoom-out analysis, like watching all horses race while betting on none


Design Principles

Based on our talks with long-time and novice stocks app users, we established the following design principles:

Familiarity: Users should be able to navigate information in a similar manner to existing tools;
Neatness: Numbers and graphs shouldn't clouder the screen
Personalization: Users should have the ability to customize their stock viewer to suit their preferences.


Information Architecture and Wireframes

We created wireframes and an information architecture that included:

Dashboard: An overview of the user's portfolio with key metrics like total value, gains/losses, and a pie chart displaying the portfolio composition. Stock Details: A dedicated page for each stock in the portfolio with real-time data, historical charts, and news. Watchlist: A section for users to track stocks they are interested in but haven't invested in yet. Prototyping We created interactive prototypes to validate the user flow and gather feedback from potential users. Testing and Iteration Usability Testing We conducted usability testing with a diverse group of users to identify pain points and areas for improvement. Key findings included:
Users appreciated the intuitive dashboard with clear visualizations. Some novice users felt overwhelmed by the amount of data, so we simplified the presentation. Experienced traders wanted advanced charting and analysis tools, which we integrated. Iterations Based on user feedback, we made several iterations to the design, including: Simplifying the initial dashboard view for novice users. Adding tooltips and explanations for complex data points. Enhancing the charting capabilities with technical analysis tools for advanced users. Final Design Visual Design The final design incorporated the app's existing aesthetic while introducing a color-coded system for stock performance and intuitive icons for actions. Features Key features of the final design included: Customizable Dashboard: Users could select the metrics and stocks displayed on their dashboard. Recommendations: The app offered stock recommendations and insights based on user preferences. Advanced Charting: Experienced users had access to advanced charting and technical analysis tools. Conclusion The Stocks Viewer feature in the Investments App is a user-centered solution that caters to a diverse user base, from novice investors to experienced traders. By offering real-time data, customization, and insights, the feature enhances the user experience and empowers users to make informed investment decisions. Future iterations may include integrating AI for more personalized recommendations and expanding to other investment asset classes.
